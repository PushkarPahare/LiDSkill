# Wortgewalt leicht gemacht: 65 Verben für den Einbürgerungstest

So your "Leben in Deutschland" test is next week. You've done the mock tests, you've gone through the 300 questions, and most of the facts are somewhere in your head. What you need now isn't another flashcard deck. It's a quick read that glues the vocabulary together, something you can skim on the train or over coffee and have the words stick.

This article covers 65 verbs. Every verb the test throws at you, organized not by grammar but by the situations where they actually show up. One chaotic day in Germany where you vote, get hired, get fired, go to court, and decorate a Christmas tree. All before dinner.

## The English bridge

Most of these verbs have cousins in English. We *elect* leaders, *pass* laws, *represent* people, *establish* institutions, *violate* rights, *file* complaints, *register* at offices. You already know the concepts. The German words just look different.

---

## 1. You wake up as a citizen

You're a German citizen. You **besitzen** (own) a Personalausweis, have had one since you turned 16. Today is election day. You **wählen** (vote) because you can, not because you must. Nobody can **zwingen** (force) you, nobody can **beeinflussen** (influence) your choice. You walk into the booth and **ankreuzen** (mark with a cross) one box on the ballot. Just one.

*Ich wähle heute.* (I'm voting today).

*Niemand darf mich zwingen.* (Nobody may force me).

Later, volunteers will **zählen** (count) the votes. The party that wins enough seats will try to **regieren** (govern). If no party has a majority alone, several will have to cooperate, and that's when they **bilden** (form) a coalition.

## 2. How laws get made

Inside the Bundestag, the Abgeordnete **beschließen** (pass) new laws. The opposition **kontrolliert** (oversees) the government.

The Bundestag also **wählt** (elects) the Bundeskanzler. But first, the Bundespräsident **schlägt vor** (proposes) a candidate. Once elected, the Bundespräsident **ernennt** (appoints) the ministers. The Kanzler **leitet** (leads) the cabinet. (If this chain confuses you, see the diagram above: Volk → Bundestag → Kanzler → Kabinett. The Bundespräsident stands to the side, proposing and appointing but never governing.)

*Der Bundestag beschließt die Gesetze.* (The Bundestag passes the laws).

*Der Bundespräsident ernennt die Minister.* (The Federal President appoints the ministers).

The Bundespräsident **repräsentiert** (represents) the country. He doesn't **regieren** (govern). That distinction comes up on the test constantly. The Bundesverfassungsgericht in Karlsruhe **legt aus** (interprets) the Grundgesetz. And if a party tries to **abschaffen** (abolish) press freedom? Can't be done. Grundrechte are not up for a vote.

*Pressefreiheit kann man nicht abschaffen.* (Press freedom cannot be abolished).

Parties that want to **errichten** (establish) a dictatorship get **verboten** (banned). The Bundesländer **entscheiden** (decide) their own school policy. Gewerkschaften **vertreten** (represent) the interests of workers.

## 3. Getting involved

Your bus route is being cancelled. You **beteiligen** sich (get involved) in a Bürgerinitiative, or you **gründen** (found) one yourself. You want to hold a demonstration? You have to **anmelden** (register) it first.

*Ich beteilige mich an einer Bürgerinitiative.* (I'm joining a citizens' initiative).

At the local level, the Bürgermeister runs the Gemeinde. Citizens **teilnehmen** (participate) in local politics. Some **wechseln** (switch) parties. It's messy. That's democracy.

## 4. Paperwork and bureaucracy

You move to a new city. First stop: Einwohnermeldeamt. You **melden sich an** (register).

*Wer umzieht, meldet sich beim Einwohnermeldeamt an.* (When you move, you register at the residents' registration office).

You want to get married? Standesamt. You **heiraten** (marry) there. You need parental allowance? You **beantragen** (apply for) it at the Elterngeldstelle. Nobody sends it to you automatically.

*Sie muss Elterngeld beantragen.* (She has to apply for parental allowance).

Your tax assessment is wrong. You **einlegen** Einspruch (file an objection). Wrong bill from another authority? You **einlegen** Widerspruch. Different words for basically the same act, but the test cares about the difference. A government employee treats you badly? You **beschweren** sich (complain) to their boss.

Want to open a restaurant? You **eröffnen** (open) it, but only after getting the Gaststättenerlaubnis. Bought a broken TV? You **reklamieren** (file a product complaint).

## 5. Hiring, firing, paying

You get a job. Your employer **stellt** you **an**. You're now fest angestellt. Every month you **zahlen** (pay) Lohnsteuer and Sozialabgaben. Your employer **zahlt** too. The costs **teilen sich** (are split) between you.

*Die Kosten teilen sich Arbeitgeber und Arbeitnehmer.* (The costs are split between employer and employee).

The state **finanziert** (finances) the Sozialversicherung through these contributions. It also **baut** (builds) roads and schools, **zahlt** (pays) Kindergeld, **unterstützt** (supports) museums, and **fördert** (funds) athletes.

Then one day your boss **kündigt** (fires) you unfairly. You **erheben** (file) a Kündigungsschutzklage at the Arbeitsgericht. If you want to quit yourself, you **beachten** (respect) the Kündigungsfrist.

*Ich erhebe eine Kündigungsschutzklage.* (I'm filing a wrongful dismissal claim).

A pregnant colleague gets **entlassen** (dismissed) because of her pregnancy? Diskriminierung. Someone gets **abgelehnt** (rejected) for a job because of their skin color? Also Diskriminierung. Someone doesn't get hired because they lack experience? Not Diskriminierung. The test asks you to tell the difference.

*Sie wurde entlassen, weil sie ein Kind bekommt.* (She was fired because she's having a baby).

## 6. Guilt and justice

Someone commits a crime and gets **angeklagt** (charged). It goes to a Prozess. The Richter **spricht** Urteile (delivers verdicts). Schöffen, the lay judges, **entscheiden** (decide) together with professional judges about guilt and punishment.

If someone **schlägt** (hits) a child, they can be **bestraft** (punished). Opening someone else's mail **verletzt** (violates) the Briefgeheimnis.

*Wer ein Kind schlägt, kann bestraft werden.* (Anyone who hits a child can be punished).

You need legal help? Rechtsanwalt. You **bewerben sich** (apply) for a job as a Schöffe? You need to be between 25 and 69.

## 7. Destruction and rebuilding

This one is dense. Bear with me.

Hitler **errichtete** (established) a dictatorship in 1933. The Nazis **verboten** (banned) parties and **zerstörten** (destroyed) synagogues. Stauffenberg tried to kill Hitler in 1944. It **scheiterte** (failed). The war ended in 1945.

After that, the Allies **teilten auf** (divided up) Germany into four zones. The Soviets **blockierten** (blocked) the land routes to West-Berlin, so the Western Allies **versorgten** (supplied) the city by air. That's the Luftbrücke.

*Die Alliierten teilten Deutschland auf.* (The Allies divided Germany up).

In 1946, KPD and SPD were forcibly **vereint** (merged) into the SED. Two states **entstanden** (were created) in 1949. Then forty years of division. In 1989, people chanted "Wir sind das Volk," the Wall fell, and the five eastern Bundesländer **traten bei** (joined) the Bundesrepublik.

*Die östlichen Bundesländer traten der Bundesrepublik bei.* (The eastern states joined the Federal Republic).

Every year on October 3, Germany **feiert** (celebrates) the Tag der Deutschen Einheit. In Verdun, Kohl and Mitterrand **gedachten** (commemorated) the dead of both World Wars. Through the Römische Verträge, states **schlossen sich zusammen** (joined together) to form the EWG. I keep mixing up *vereinen* and *zusammenschließen*, honestly. The first one feels forced (it was), the second one voluntary (it was). That's the distinction.

## 8. Believing, learning, celebrating

In Germany, everyone can **glauben** (believe) what they want. Christianity has **geprägt** (shaped) the culture. Parents can **entscheiden** (decide) about Religionsunterricht until their child turns 14.

You have to keep **lernen** (learning) even after your Ausbildung. At Easter, you **bemalen** (paint) eggs. At Christmas, you **schmücken** (decorate) the Tannenbaum. In the 1950s, the Bundesrepublik **warb an** (recruited) Gastarbeiter. The first ones came from Italy.

*Zu Ostern bemalt man Eier.* (At Easter, you paint eggs).

This branch has the fewest verbs but the test still asks about it. Don't skip it.

## Summary

65 verbs. Eight branches. One fake day.

The ones that come up most: **wählen**, **beschließen**, **ernennen**, **vertreten**, **abschaffen**, **verbieten**, **anmelden**, **beantragen**, **einlegen**, **erheben**, **entscheiden**, **beitreten**, **feststellen**. If you know those cold, the rest will follow.

Prost!
